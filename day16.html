Part 02

聽力練習

Listening & Dialogue
Dialogue — 文化差異與禮儀 / Culture & Social Etiquette
Host
"Welcome to our home! Please, come in and make yourself at home." 歡迎來到我們家！請進，把這裡當自己家。
Guest
"Thank you so much. You have a beautiful home." 非常感謝。您的家真是漂亮。
Host
"Can I get you something to drink? Tea, coffee, or juice?" 我可以給您準備點喝的嗎？茶、咖啡還是果汁？
Guest
"Tea would be lovely, thank you. I hope I'm not imposing." 茶就很好了，謝謝。希望我沒有打擾到您。
Host
"Not at all! We're so happy you could visit." 完全沒有！我們很高興您能來拜訪。
Guest
"I brought some pastries from the bakery. I hope you like them." 我帶了一些來自麵包店的糕點。希望您喜歡。
Host
"Oh, you shouldn't have! That's very kind of you!" 哦，你真是太客氣了！你真好心！

Part 04

閱讀理解

Reading Comprehension
Reading Passage
Cultural etiquette varies greatly around the English-speaking world. In the United Kingdom, queueing (standing in line) is taken very seriously, and cutting in line is considered extremely rude. In Australia, people tend to be casual and egalitarian — you might call your boss by their first name. In the United States, it is common to smile at strangers and engage in brief small talk. When invited to someone's home in most Western cultures, it is polite to bring a small gift such as wine, flowers, or chocolates. Never arrive early — arriving exactly on time or a few minutes late is perfectly acceptable in many countries.
I'm afraid + negative news | I don't want to impose, but... | You shouldn't have! (謝人時的套語) | I really appreciate + noun/it
'I'm afraid' 用於禮貌地傳達壞消息或拒絕：'I'm afraid I can't come.' 這個表達非常有禮，遠比直接說 'I can't come.' 更得體。
