Part 02

聽力練習

Listening & Dialogue
Dialogue — 工作與職場 / Work & Professional Settings
Manager
"Good morning, everyone. Let's get started. First item on the agenda?" 早安，各位。我們開始吧。議程的第一項是？
Employee
"We need to discuss the Q3 marketing budget." 我們需要討論第三季行銷預算。
Manager
"Right. What's the current status?" 好的。目前狀況如何？
Employee
"We're ten percent under budget, which gives us some flexibility." 我們比預算少用了百分之十，這給了我們一些彈性。
Manager
"Excellent. I'd like to allocate that to the digital campaign. Any concerns?" 很好。我想把那筆錢分配給數位行銷。有任何疑慮嗎？
Employee
"I'd like to raise a concern about the timeline. We may need more time." 我想提出對時間表的疑慮。我們可能需要更多時間。
Manager
"Fair point. Let's discuss the timeline. What are the next steps?" 說得有道理。我們來討論時間表。接下來的步驟是什麼？

Part 04

閱讀理解

Reading Comprehension
Reading Passage
Professional English communication is characterized by clarity, politeness, and efficiency. In meetings, it is important to let others finish speaking before you contribute. Use phrases like 'Building on what you said...' or 'To add to that point...' to show you are listening. When writing professional emails, use a clear subject line and keep the email concise. Begin with a greeting, state your purpose in the first sentence, and end with a clear call to action or next step. Avoid using casual language, slang, or emojis in professional emails unless you have established a very informal relationship with the recipient.
I'd like to + action (softer) | We may/might + verb (possibility) | Could you + request? | To follow up on + topic...
職場英語強調 'hedging language'（緩和語氣）：'I'd like to raise a concern' 比 'There's a problem!' 更專業；'We may need more time' 比 'We're going to be late' 更得體。
