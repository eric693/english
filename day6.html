Part 02

聽力練習

Listening & Dialogue
Dialogue — 購物殺價 / Shopping & Bargaining
Customer
"Excuse me, how much is this scarf?" 打擾一下，這條圍巾多少錢？
Seller
"It's forty-five dollars." 四十五美元。
Customer
"That's a bit expensive. Could you do thirty?" 有點貴。可以算三十嗎？
Seller
"I can do thirty-eight. That's my best price." 我可以算三十八。這是最低價了。
Customer
"Do you have it in red?" 有紅色的嗎？
Seller
"Yes! Let me get it for you." 有！我去拿給你。
Customer
"Great, I'll take the red one for thirty-eight." 好的，我買那條三十八的紅色。

Part 04

閱讀理解

Reading Comprehension
Reading Passage
Shopping is one of the most common activities for travelers. In markets and small shops around the world, bargaining is expected and even considered fun. However, in department stores and supermarkets, prices are usually fixed. When shopping for clothes, it helps to know your size in different systems: US, UK, and EU sizes differ. Always ask if you can try something on before buying. Keep your receipts in case you need to return or exchange an item. The return policy in English-speaking countries is usually quite flexible.
Could you do + price? | Can you give me a discount? | That's too expensive. | I'll take it. / I'll pass.
條件句用於談價錢：'Could you do thirty?' = 你能算三十嗎？比直接說 'Give me thirty!' 有禮貌得多，更容易成功殺價。
